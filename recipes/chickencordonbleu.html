Chicken Cordon Bleu

Chicken Cordon Bleu—or as the French call it, Chicken Cordon Bleu—is an entree where chicken is wrapped around cheese and ham, baked, and then topped with a creamy mustard-tinged sauce.

The French stands for "blue ribbon," I believe.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast halves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 6 slices Swiss cheese
  • 4 slices cooked ham
  • 1/2 cup seasoned bread crumbs

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Coat a baking dish with oil or cooking spray.
  2. Pound chicken breast halves to 1/4 inch thickness.
  3. Season chicken with salt and pepper. Place a Swiss cheese slice and a ham slice on top of each chicken breast.
  4. Roll each chicken breast and secure with toothpicks.
  5. Place chicken breasts in the baking dish and sprinkle bread crumbs over the top.
  6. Bake in the oven until chicken is no longer pink—30 to 35 minutes.
  7. Remove from the oven and place 1/2 Swiss cheese slice on top of each chicken breast.
  8. Return to the oven for 3 to 5 minutes, until cheese has melted. Remove toothpicks and serve.
